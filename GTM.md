# Go-to-Market (GTM) Strategy

### Exact Target User
Our exact target user is a Series A to Series C Startup Founder, Chief Technology Officer (CTO), or VP of Engineering. They manage teams of 15 to 150 engineers. They care deeply about runway, capital efficiency, and giving their developers the best tools, but they lack the time to manually audit every credit card charge or software invoice. 

### What They Search / Scroll
Before realizing they need SpendLens AI, these leaders are searching for:
- "Cursor vs GitHub Copilot ROI"
- "How to reduce Anthropic API bills"
- "Developer productivity metrics"
- "Engineering budget templates 2026"
They are passively scrolling through discussions about AI tool consolidation on Hacker News, X (formerly Twitter), and specialized engineering leadership newsletters.

### Specific Online Communities
We will target:
1. **r/ExperiencedDevs and r/CTO on Reddit**: High concentration of engineering leaders discussing stack choices.
2. **Y Combinator Hacker News (Show HN)**: The perfect audience for an engineering-focused financial tool.
3. **Private Slack/Discord Communities**: e.g., Rands Leadership Slack, CTO Craft, and local startup incubator alumni channels.

### First 100 Users in 30 Days ($0 Budget)
To acquire the first 100 users without spending money:
1. **The "Show HN" Launch**: A well-crafted Show HN post focusing on the technical deterministic engine and the absurd overlaps we've found in developer spending. A successful post easily drives 2,000+ targeted visitors, yielding our 100 users.
2. **Direct Cold Outreach via LinkedIn**: Search for "CTO" at companies that recently raised a Series A. Send a highly personalized message: *"Noticed [Company] is scaling the engineering team. I built a free tool that scans your AI stack for redundant seats (e.g., paying for both Copilot and Cursor). Takes 60 seconds."*
3. **Engineering Newsletter Features**: Reach out to authors of newsletters like *Pragmatic Engineer* or *ByteByteGo* offering them early access to aggregated, anonymized data on "What AI tools startups are actually paying for in 2026" in exchange for a link to the tool.

### Unfair Distribution Channel
Our unfair distribution channel is **Open Source & Transparency**. By making the pricing logic and the application itself open-source (or highly transparent), we earn the trust of deeply skeptical, highly technical engineering leaders who usually ignore traditional B2B SaaS marketing.

### Week-1 Traction Metrics
Success in Week 1 is defined by:
- **1,500 Unique Visitors** to the landing page.
- **15% Audit Completion Rate** (225 audits run).
- **10% Email Capture Rate** (22 saved reports/leads generated).
- At least **2 High-Value Leads** (>$10k/yr in savings identified) handed off to Credex for consultation.
